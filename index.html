About CodeQuestt​ '25

CodeQuestt is an annual hackathon designed for students in Grades 8 to 12, aiming to foster creativity, problem-solving, and technical skills. Whether you're a beginner or a seasoned coder, CodeQuestt provides an opportunity to work in teams or solo to tackle innovative challenges.

Hackathon Flow

For first time hackathon attendees, here's how you make an impressive idea, and present it.

For first-time hackathon participants, focus on identifying a clear problem to solve and ensure your idea is simple and feasible within the time limit. Leverage your team's strengths and conduct quick research on existing solutions. When presenting, structure your pitch clearly—cover the problem, your solution, and its potential impact. Use storytelling to engage your audience and include a live demo if possible. Practice thoroughly and be ready to answer questions from the judges to demonstrate your understanding and commitment. Good luck!

The hackathon flow typically begins with an opening ceremony, where participants are welcomed and given an overview of the event. After the introduction, teams form and brainstorm project ideas, followed by the coding phase, which lasts for 48 hours. During this period, teams develop their projects, often seeking mentorship and guidance. As the deadline approaches, teams prepare their final presentations. The event culminates in a demo day, where each team showcases their work to judges and fellow participants. Prizes are awarded based on innovation, functionality, and presentation quality, concluding with a closing ceremony that celebrates all participants.
